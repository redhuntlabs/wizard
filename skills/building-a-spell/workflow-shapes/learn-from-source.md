# Shape: Learn From Source

## Trigger keywords

learn from, take notes on, study, understand, dig into, get the gist, course notes, book notes, paper notes

## Output type

Notes on a source you can refer back to without re-reading the source.

## Strawman

```
Inputs:
  - The source
  - Why you're reading it (the question you want answered)
  - Your existing knowledge level (novice / intermediate / expert)

Steps:
  1. Skim for structure (headings, abstracts, intros)
  2. Read with one eye on your question
  3. Capture: the source's central claim in your words
  4. Capture: 3 facts you didn't know
  5. Capture: 1 thing that contradicts something you believed
  6. Capture: 1 question this source raised but didn't answer
  7. Tag with how this connects to existing notes (if any)

Quality bar:
  - Notes stand alone (you wouldn't need the source to use them)
  - Step 5 found a real contradiction, or you didn't read closely enough
  - Step 6 is specific (a question, not "more research needed")
```

## Common variations

- **Paper for research**: add "methods I might borrow" section
- **Book for skill-building**: add "first thing to try" section
- **Lecture / video**: add timestamps to each note
